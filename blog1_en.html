A volunteer's confession about the bag of stones we all carry through life

Author: Ostatnia Warta Volunteer | Date: February 2026

Some decisions in life are not born out of a plan. They do not appear at a desk, in a calendar, or in an Excel spreadsheet. They are born in silence. In that special silence that remains after someone close passes away.

Sometimes I think that every person goes through life with a bag slung over their shoulder. We throw unspoken words, missed opportunities, regrets, and fears into it. When we are healthy and full of strength, we don't feel this weight. We just march forward.

Mr. Andrzej, with whom I sat last night in the hospice, had a very heavy bag.

"It wasn't the grip of someone who wants to talk about the weather. It was the grip of someone who is afraid to close their eyes."

He didn't say much. Shallow breath, eyes fixed on a single point on the ceiling. But when I grabbed his thin, cold hand, I felt his grip tighten. It wasn't the grip of someone who wants to talk about the weather. It was the grip of someone who is afraid to close their eyes.

We sat like that for four hours. He didn't ask my name. I didn't ask about his life. At "Ostatnia Warta", we quickly learn that words are sometimes overrated. Presence carries more weight.

When his breathing became calmer towards the morning, and his hand finally relaxed, I thought about his bag of stones. We, the volunteers, cannot take these bags off. We cannot turn back time. We will not cure cancer.

But for those few hours, we can help carry it. So that for a moment, at the very end, the burden doesn't crush so much.

That is why I do this. That is why I come. Because I know that one day, my bag will also become too heavy for me to carry alone.
