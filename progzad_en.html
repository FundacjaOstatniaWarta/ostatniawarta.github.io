Facing a sentence? Everyone has the right to make amends.

Criminal proceedings (e.g., for driving under the influence under Art. 178a of the Penal Code) are incredibly stressful. However, before the verdict is passed, you have the opportunity to demonstrate to the court your pro-social attitude, regret for the act committed, and willingness to make amends.

One of the most effective ways to show the court that you want to rectify your mistake is to make a voluntary donation to a public benefit organization.

How does voluntary redress affect the sentence?

According to Art. 53 § 2 of the Polish Penal Code, when imposing a penalty, the court takes into account, among other things, the perpetrator's behavior after the crime was committed, in particular, their efforts to repair the damage or to make amends in another form to the public sense of justice.

By making a donation and attaching the official certificate to your case files, you provide your defense attorney with a strong argument for applying an extraordinarily mitigated penalty, a suspended sentence, or a conditional discontinuance of the proceedings.
