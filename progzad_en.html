How to reduce the penalty for driving under the influence – a proactive attitude before the court

Being arrested for driving under the influence of alcohol (Art. 178a § 1 of the Penal Code) causes immense stress and the specter of severe consequences. However, before you hear the court's verdict, you have a chance to prove that you have learned your lesson. According to Polish law, the defendant can take proactive measures, which the Court may take into account when determining the penalty.

The Ostatnia Warta Foundation accepts payments for court penalties and voluntary monetary contributions, and issues official certificates for case files.

What are the penalties for driving under the influence?

Polish law provides for severe consequences, including:

Fine or imprisonment

A heavy fine, restriction of liberty, and in extreme cases, even imprisonment.

Driving ban

The court obligatorily imposes a driving ban for a period ranging from 3 to even 15 years.

Forfeiture of property

The possibility of ordering the forfeiture of the motor vehicle or imposing an obligation to pay its equivalent value.

Monetary penalty

A mandatory financial contribution to the Justice Fund ranging from 5,000 PLN to 60,000 PLN.

Can you influence the sentence before the hearing?

Yes. According to Art. 53 § 2 of the Penal Code, when determining the penalty, the court considers not only the incident itself but also the perpetrator's behavior after committing the crime. This means that your attitude—remorse, admitting your mistake, and a desire to make amends to society—can have a real impact on whether the Court applies a more lenient sentence (e.g., from the lower limits of the threatened penalty).

One of the most effective actions, often advised by defense attorneys, is voluntarily supporting a social organization. Making a donation for statutory purposes to the Ostatnia Warta Foundation sends a clear signal to the court: "I made a mistake, but I am taking responsibility for it and I want to repair the social harm."

Important: You must take this action before the verdict is issued. It is a voluntary act of redress, independent of any subsequent, mandatory penalties imposed by the court.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

A big TV foundation vs. a smaller organization – which is viewed better by the court?

For the court, the size of the organization or its advertising budget doesn't matter; what matters is the proactive, pro-social attitude of the defendant and the reliability of the documentation. Lawyers often advise choosing smaller, transparent foundations (like "Ostatnia Warta") because they support the most difficult, underfunded areas (e.g., caring for the dying). Additionally, smaller organizations act faster – in our case, you have a guarantee that a complete, error-free document with seals will reach you within 24 hours, whereas in large foundations, waiting for a certificate can sometimes take weeks.

Where to pay the monetary penalty for driving under the influence?

The mandatory financial penalty from a verdict is paid to the state Justice Fund indicated by the court. However, before the verdict, you can make a voluntary donation to the "Ostatnia Warta" Foundation to help obtain a more lenient sentence.

Driving hungover – will a donation to the foundation mitigate my penalty?

The court takes into account the desire to repair the harm and the defendant's attitude (Art. 53 § 2 of the Penal Code). Regardless of whether it was a conscious mistake or so-called "unconscious intoxication", a voluntary payment for social purposes is an extremely strong argument for your defense attorney.

How much is the monetary penalty for driving under the influence?

The judicial financial penalty for driving in a state of intoxication starts from a minimum of 5,000 PLN and can amount up to 60,000 PLN.

Does voluntary redress replace the court's verdict?

No. It is a proactive action meant to convince the court that you have drawn conclusions before the end of the trial, which significantly facilitates applying for a lower penalty or conditional discontinuance.

Can the court order a payment to a specific foundation?

For crimes under Art. 178a of the Penal Code, the funds adjudicated by the sentence automatically go to the state fund. That is why your voluntary, prior action before this sentence is imposed is so crucial.

Caught driving under the influence – what to do immediately after the arrest?

Consult a lawyer immediately and start proactive remedial actions. Making a donation and presenting an official certificate from the Foundation in court is one of the best, proven first steps.

How to document the donation for the court?

First-time DUI – what are the consequences and how to help my case?

Even for the first arrest, the penal code is merciless – you face losing your driver's license, an entry in the criminal record, a very high fine, and a penal contribution. It is always worth fighting for the lowest penalty and the court's favor by taking steps such as voluntary redress before the hearing.

Does the foundation's certificate help with conditional discontinuance of proceedings?

Yes! When applying for a conditional discontinuance of criminal proceedings, demonstrating the defendant's pro-social attitude (e.g., supporting our foundation helping the terminally ill) is an absolutely crucial argument.

Does the donation amount matter to the court?

Yes, the court evaluates your commitment. Symbolic payments (e.g., 50 PLN) may be treated instrumentally and even backfire. Proportional and tangible support shows a real desire to make amends and learn from the mistake.
